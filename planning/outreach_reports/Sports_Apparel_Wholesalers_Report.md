## 3. Tips for Approaching Wholesalers

To maximize your success when reaching out to these wholesalers and distributors, keep these strategies in mind:

1.  **Lead with Your Capabilities (OEM/ODM):** Distributors often look for partners who can handle the "heavy lifting" of design and production. Explicitly state that you offer "white-label" or "custom manufacturing" services.
2.  **Focus on Speed:** The sports industry is highly seasonal. Highlighting your **fast turnaround times** (e.g., "3-week delivery from design approval") is a major selling point for buyers who need to restock quickly.
3.  **Offer Samples Early:** Professional buyers are skeptical of photos. Proactively offering to send a **sample pack** shows confidence in your quality and reduces the perceived risk of starting a new partnership.
4.  **Emphasize "Fit & Function":** In sports like BJJ or Football, the technical aspect of the garment (breathability, stretch, durability) is more important than aesthetics. Use technical terms like "sublimation printing," "moisture-wicking," and "reinforced stitching" in your pitches.
5.  **Personalize the Hook:** Mention a specific sport or category they are currently underserved in. For example, if a teamwear distributor lacks a strong hockey line, tailor your pitch to your hockey apparel expertise.
